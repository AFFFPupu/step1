Differentiation

Differentiation measures how a function changes as its input changes.

  • Physical Interpretation: Rate of change, slope of tangent, velocity, acceleration, etc.
  • Mathematical Definition (First Principle):
    f′(x)=lim⁡h→0f(x+h)−f(x)h.f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h}.f′(x)=h→0lim​hf(x+h)−f(x)​.

Key Applications:

  • Rate of change problems
  • Optimization
  • Motion (velocity, acceleration)
  • Tangent lines

Notes:

First Principle of Differentiation

f′(x)=lim⁡h→0f(x+h)−f(x)h.f'(x)=\lim _{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}.f′(x)=h→0lim​hf(x+h)−f(x)​.

  • Interpretation: Instantaneous rate of change or slope of the tangent line at a point.
  • Examples:
    • Velocity = derivative of position.
    • Acceleration = derivative of velocity.

Differentiation Rules

Chain Rule

For h(x)=g(f(x))h(x) = g(f(x))h(x)=g(f(x)):
h′(x)=g′(f(x))⋅f′(x).h'(x) = g'(f(x)) \cdot f'(x).h′(x)=g′(f(x))⋅f′(x).

Example: ddx[sin⁡(x2)]=cos⁡(x2)⋅2x.\frac{d}{dx}[\sin(x^2)] = \cos(x^2) \cdot 2x.dxd​[sin(x2)]=cos(x2)⋅2x.

Product Rule

ddx[u⋅v]=udvdx+vdudx.\frac{d}{dx}[u \cdot v] = u \frac{dv}{dx} + v \frac{du}{dx}.dxd​[u⋅v]=udxdv​+vdxdu​.

Example: ddx[xsin⁡x]=sin⁡x+xcos⁡x.\frac{d}{dx}[x \sin x] = \sin x + x \cos x.dxd​[xsinx]=sinx+xcosx.

Quotient Rule

ddx(uv)=v dudx−u dvdxv2.\frac{d}{dx}\left(\frac{u}{v}\right) = \frac{v\,\frac{du}{dx} - u\,\frac{dv}{dx}}{v^2}.dxd​(vu​)=v2vdxdu​−udxdv​​.

Example: ddx[xsin⁡x]=sin⁡x⋅1−x⋅cos⁡x(sin⁡x)2.\frac{d}{dx}\left[\frac{x}{\sin x}\right] = \frac{\sin x \cdot 1 - x \cdot \cos x}{(\sin x)^2}.dxd​[sinxx​]=(sinx)2sinx⋅1−x⋅cosx​.
